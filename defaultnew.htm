Why is THE COMMUNICATION PROJECT important to us? What do we get out of working on our writing? Why do we do it?

A COMMUNICATION PROJECT student answers these questions:

Our writing is very important to me and to my peers. Our writing gives us a chance to express our thoughts and our feelings without someone arguing with our peace of mind or telling us what is right and what is wrong. Our writing gives us an understanding of one another. Most kids need to express themselves to thrive in our society today.

To the adults who read our writing, we want you to understand us better. Our writing is mostly on kids, on what goes through kids� heads, and why we do what we do.

I believe it is important for all people to understand and acknowledge our existence.

I write to be understood.

 
 

The New York State Literary Center (NYSLC) is a Rochester, New York based Arts in Education organization that through THE COMMUNICATION PROJECT serves adolescents at the highest risk for educational failure in schools, in day treatment and residential placement programs, in group homes, in long term suspension programs, and in juvenile justice facilities through interdisciplinary, collaborative, arts integrated programs where writing is used to improve not only basic literacy skills, but to inject a sense of community belonging, to give students power over the narrative of their own lives, to reach out with strong, clear voices on personal and social identity, and to articulate a compelling vision of how communities and schools can be better places. The NYSLC is committed to deepening adolescents understanding of the forces that shape their lives and to help adults better understand the needs and concerns of all children.

The NYSLC believes in the potential of all young people and believes that hip-hop is one of the greatest and most exciting art forms of our time. The NYSLC connects the rhyme book to the classroom.
