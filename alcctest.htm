The premise of Arts, Literacy, and The Classroom Community (ALCC), The New York State Literary Center�s partnership with the Rochester City School District�s Youth and Justice Program, in collaboration with the Office of The Sheriff, County of Monroe, at the Monroe County Jail and the Monroe Correctional Facility, is teaching and learning through the arts for incarcerated adolescents, a population research has demonstrated is at the highest risk for educational failure. The partnership serves incarcerated adolescents, 15 to 19, who have been tried and convicted as adults and are serving sentences of one year or under; and adolescents, 15 to 19, who have been arrested and are awaiting trial. The goals of the Partnership are:

  • To develop strength-based arts strategies to motivate the reluctant learner.

  • To reinforce basic literacy.

  • To provide opportunities to build competencies needed for successful transition.

  • To motivate positive behavior.

  • To develop critical and analytical thinking skills.

  • To expand horizons.

  • To develop a sense of self, well being, and community belonging.

The focus of Arts, Literacy, and The Classroom Community is the creation of a classroom community / learning space that motivates reluctant learners, motivates positive behavior, meets the academic needs of a transient population, builds competencies for successful transition, and offers incarcerated adolescents positive and productive options.
